The Stomach

Overview

The stomach is a muscular organ located on the left side of the upper abdomen. It plays a vital role in digestion by breaking down food into smaller particles using acids and enzymes. The stomach prepares food for absorption in the small intestine.

Structure of the Stomach

The stomach is divided into several parts, each serving specific functions:

  • Cardia: The entry point where food passes from the esophagus into the stomach.
  • Fundus: The upper rounded portion that stores undigested food and gases released during digestion.
  • Body: The largest section where most digestion occurs.
  • Pylorus: The lower part that connects to the small intestine and regulates the passage of digested food.

Functions of the Stomach

The stomach has several key functions:

  • Digestion: The stomach uses hydrochloric acid and digestive enzymes to break down proteins and food particles.
  • Storage: It stores food temporarily before passing it to the small intestine.
  • Secretion: Produces gastric juices and mucus to aid digestion and protect the stomach lining.
  • Churning: The stomach's muscular walls mix and churn food for better digestion.

Interesting Facts About the Stomach

  • The stomach can expand to hold up to 4 liters of food and liquid.
  • Its lining regenerates every few days to protect it from the acidic environment.
  • Hydrochloric acid in the stomach is strong enough to dissolve metal.
  • The stomach produces about 3 liters of gastric juice daily.

Health Tips for the Stomach

To keep your stomach healthy, follow these tips:

  • Eat balanced meals and avoid overeating.
  • Limit spicy, fatty, and acidic foods if you experience heartburn.
  • Stay hydrated to support digestion.
  • Avoid lying down immediately after eating to prevent acid reflux.
  • Incorporate probiotics to maintain a healthy gut microbiome.
